Joint Cosmology & Particle Physics Meeting

Wednesday, May 27th, 2026 · Center for Theoretical Physics, Columbia University

About

The Joint Cosmology & Particle Physics Meeting is a regional workshop that was once held regularly before the pandemic, bringing together faculty, post-docs, and graduate students alike from Columbia, NYU, and UPenn. We will hear a few talks from the students and post docs and discuss current topics at the intersection of high-energy particle physics and cosmology.

This meeting comes at an opportune time since there has been a large growth in the use of techniques developed in the realm of scattering amplitudes to questions in gravitational physics. Meanwhile, questions and strategies developed to tackle questions in gravity have helped advance our understanding of scattering amplitudes and their generalizations. The talks are intended to be accessible to the broader community and to encourage discussion across our different subfields.

Attendance is free for all students, post docs, and faculty at the aforementioned institutions. Registration is requested for planning purposes, as we will provide lunch and you will need a QR code to enter the Columbia campus. If there are any questions of concerns, please send an email to jrs2277@columbia.edu. To register, please sign up on this google form.
